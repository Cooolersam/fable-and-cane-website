About Fable and Cane

Print knowledge. Preserve forests.

The Problem

The publishing industry depends on wood pulp and petroleum-based inks — a model built on deforestation, pollution, and waste. Meanwhile, sugarcane bagasse, the fibrous material left after sugar extraction, is burned or sent to landfill by the millions of tons every year.

The Idea

We started Fable and Cane with a simple idea:

A book should store knowledge without destroying forests.

What if we could turn agricultural waste into beautiful, durable books? What if every page could represent carbon stored rather than carbon emitted?

The Solution

We developed FiberCane paper — turning that agricultural waste into book-quality pages. Paired with soy-based inks and covers made from recycled water bottles, every material in a Fable and Cane book is chosen to reduce harm and extend the product's life.

The result: a book that's identical in quality to traditional options, priced the same, but built on a circular model where nothing goes to waste and no forests are cut.

Our Mission

Print knowledge. Preserve forests.

Every book we publish is proof that sustainability and quality are not in conflict. We believe the publishing industry can lead the way toward a circular, low-carbon economy -- one page at a time.

We price our books identically to traditional options because we believe sustainable choices should be accessible to everyone, not just a premium market.

Our Values

Transparency

We disclose every material, process, and environmental impact. No greenwashing -- just science.

Circularity

Every component is chosen for its ability to be reused, recycled, or composted at end of life.

Quality

Sustainable materials should never mean compromised quality. Our books meet professional publishing standards.

Impact

We measure success not just in sales, but in trees saved, carbon stored, and waste diverted.
