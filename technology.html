Our Technology

The materials and processes behind every CaneLeaf book.

Internal Pages

70% Bagasse Fiber
30% Recycled Pulp

Our internal pages blend sugarcane bagasse fiber with post-consumer recycled wood pulp. This combination maintains the strength, brightness, and printability expected from professional publishing while minimizing forest impact.

Bagasse fibers are shorter than wood fibers, so blending with recycled pulp ensures structural integrity. The result is a smooth, bright page that performs identically to traditional paper in printing and binding.

Ultra Low VOC Soy Ink

Less Air Pollution

Soy-based inks produce significantly fewer volatile organic compounds (VOCs) during printing compared to petroleum-based inks.

Easier Paper Recycling

Soy ink releases more easily from paper fibers during the de-inking process, resulting in higher quality recycled paper.

Safer Printing Process

Reduced exposure to harmful chemicals for printing press operators and facility workers.

Circular Plastic Lamination

Our Library Durability covers use Post-Consumer Recycled Polyester (rPET) made from recycled water bottles. This lamination layer provides:

Stronger Books

Lamination protects covers from moisture, tearing, and general wear.

Longer Lifespan (10-15 Years)

Durable covers mean fewer replacement copies, reducing overall resource consumption.

Reduced Plastic Waste

Every laminated cover diverts plastic bottles from landfills and oceans.

End-of-Life Recycling

Our lamination system is designed for high-heat delamination. During industrial recycling, the plastic film separates cleanly from the paper fiber at elevated temperatures, allowing both materials to be recycled independently.

Future Development

We are exploring PLA film made from plant starch or sugarcane ethylene as an alternative to rPET lamination. PLA provides similar durability while being biodegradable under industrial composting conditions. This would create a fully plant-based book from cover to page.
