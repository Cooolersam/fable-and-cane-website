Traditional Books vs. Fable and Cane

Our product reimagines every part of the book — here's how it stacks up.

Traditional Publishing

Environmental

  • Relies on virgin wood pulp from harvested forests
  • Contributes to deforestation of 4 billion trees/year globally
  • Petroleum-based inks release volatile organic compounds (VOCs) into the atmosphere
  • Plastic lamination is made from new fossil fuel-derived materials

Economic

  • Paper costs tied to volatile timber markets
  • Logging operations often in remote, high-cost areas
  • Bagasse waste from sugar production is burned or sent to landfill — zero economic value captured

Ethical

  • Logging displaces indigenous communities and wildlife habitats
  • Petroleum ink production exposes workers to harmful chemical fumes
  • Single-use plastic covers contribute to ocean and landfill pollution

Fable and Cane

Environmental

  • 70% sugarcane bagasse (agricultural waste) + 30% recycled pulp — zero trees cut
  • Sugarcane captures 22 tons of CO2 per hectare/year via C4 photosynthesis; carbon stays locked in the book for decades
  • Soy-based inks produce dramatically fewer VOCs and are easier to de-ink for recycling
  • Covers use rPET lamination from recycled water bottles, diverting plastic from landfills

Economic

  • Sugarcane farmers earn dual revenue — selling sugar and fiber from the same harvest
  • Converts a waste stream into a valuable raw material, creating new rural jobs
  • Same retail price as traditional books — sustainability at no extra cost to the consumer

Ethical

  • No deforestation means forests remain intact as habitats for biodiversity and indigenous communities
  • Soy ink is safer for print workers — fewer toxic fumes in production facilities
  • Every component is designed for end-of-life recycling through high-heat delamination — nothing goes to waste
