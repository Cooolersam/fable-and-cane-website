Why Sugarcane Paper?

Scroll to explore our sustainability features

Faster Growing Resource

Sugarcane grows in about 12 months, compared to 10-30 years for trees. This means a rapidly renewable paper source.

Circular Waste Use

Our paper uses bagasse, the leftover fiber after sugar extraction. This waste would otherwise be burned or discarded.

Forest Conservation

Using bagasse instead of wood pulp saves millions of trees each year. Forests remain standing as carbon sinks and biodiversity habitats.

Carbon Storage

Fast-growing sugarcane captures carbon rapidly. When turned into books that last decades, this carbon becomes a long-term carbon sink.

Low Pollution Printing

We use soy-based inks, reducing VOC emissions and improving recycling. Better for printers, readers, and the planet.
